Beaches are fascinating places! They are dynamic landscapes where land meets water, typically along coastlines. The key features of a beach include: Sand: Most beaches have sandy shores, although some may have pebbles, rocks, or even mud. Sand is formed from the erosion of rocks and minerals and is often composed of tiny grains of quartz, feldspar, and other minerals. Waves: The movement of water creates waves that shape the beach. Waves can vary in size and strength depending on the weather, tides, and geographical location. Tides: The regular rise and fall of sea levels, caused by the gravitational pull of the moon and the sun, influence the beach environment. Tides affect the extent of the beach and can expose different areas at low tide

These particles are typically the result of the erosion of rocks and coral or shells washed up by the tides.
